Step-by-Step Guide

The 13-Step Setup Journey

Every screen of the tool, explained in plain language. Walk through the complete setup from trading style selection to backtesting dashboards.

🧑‍💼 Identity & Engine Setup
Steps 1 · 2A · 2B · 2C — Trading Style, Engine Mode, Combo Builder & Indicator Parameters
Steps 1 · 2A · 2B · 2C

Trading Style, Engine Mode, Combo Builder & Indicator Parameters

  • Step 1 — Choose Intraday or Positional trading style. Select Trade Mode: Long Only, Short Only, or Both. For NIFTY/BANKNIFTY options, Intraday with Both directions is recommended.
  • Step 2A — Select the Primary Engine Mode. ChAlgoX Hybrid uses all sub-signals together. You can also run pure EMA Crossover, Zone B/R, RSI, BB, SuperTrend, VWAP, or build a Custom Combo.
  • Step 2B — The Custom Combo Builder lets you pick exactly which sub-signals fire entries: Smart EMA Cross, Zone Breakout/Breakdown, Zone Reversal, EMA Pullback, Trend Continuation, Momentum Flip, RSI OB/OS Cross, BB Band Break, SuperTrend Flip, VWAP Cross — any combination you choose.
  • Step 2C — Fine-tune the Above Listed Indicators Parameters Settings: EMA Fast Period (9) / Slow Period (21), RSI Overbought (65) / Oversold (35), Bollinger Band Period (20) / Multiplier (2), and SuperTrend ATR (10) / Factor (3). All defaults are optimised for 3-minute NIFTY charts.
  • 💡 Tip: Enable Auto-Apply Best Settings for NIFTY50 to let the engine self-tune when you're on the NIFTY chart.
📊 AMD Smart Zones
Step 3 — What Are My Supports & Resistance Zones? (AMD Smart Zones)
Step 3

What Are My Supports & Resistance Zones? (AMD Smart Zones)

  • Step 3 — The engine builds AMD Smart Zones (Accumulation–Manipulation–Distribution) automatically from historical session data. Three zone types available:
  • Core Zone B/R — the opening band (blue zone) that defines the initial range. Reaction B/R — upper/lower reaction zones (UM/LM). Expansion B/R — upper/lower expansion zones (UD/LD).
  • Set the Zone Calculation Window (09:15–15:15 for NSE), Historical lookback (50 sessions), and Mode (Auto). The zones repaint fresh every morning.
  • Configure Sensitivity (0.25 default) and Core Width ATR (0.05 default). Core Width ATR controls how tight the blue opening band is — start at 0.05 and widen only in high-volatility weeks.
  • 💡 Tip: Keep all three zone types enabled for complete market structure visibility. The Core Zone is your primary reference for sideways protection.
🚨 Avoid Trading & Gap Protection
Steps 5A · 5B · 6 — Avoid Trading Rules, SMC Integration & Opening Gap Protection
Steps 5A · 5B · 6

When Should I Avoid Trading, SMC Integration & Market Opening Fear

  • Step 5A — When Should I Avoid Trading? Configure Confirm Bars (3) to make the engine wait 3 candles after a zone breakout before triggering, reducing false breakout traps. Enable Confirm at Candle Close for extra safety.
  • Enable ADX Trend Filter with ADX Min Level (25 default) to avoid trading in sideways markets. Core Zone Block prevents trades inside the blue opening band. EMA Slope Strength (0.02) ensures EMAs have meaningful separation.
  • Restrict to Signal Window with Market Direction Window (09:20–14:45 default) blocks signals outside optimal trading hours.
  • Step 5B — Smart Money Concepts (SMC) Integration: Enable Use SMC Structure as Compulsory Filter, BOS Filter (Break of Structure), FVG (Fair Value Gap), and OB SMC Swing (20) for Order Block detection.
  • Step 6 — How Should I Handle Market Opening Fear? Enable Opening Gap Protection with Gap Threshold % (3 default) — if the market opens with a gap larger than the threshold, all signals are blocked. Wait at Market Open with Wait Bars After Open (1) blocks the first candle entirely.
  • 💡 Tip: Keep Gap Threshold at 0.5–1% for NIFTY. Increase to 2–3% only if you want to trade gap-up/gap-down days deliberately.
💪 Direction Alignment Filter
Step 4 — How To Make Direction Indicator Strong?
Step 4

How To Make Direction Indicator Strong? (Direction Alignment Filter)

  • Step 4 — The Direction Alignment Filter scores each signal using multiple factors. Enable the filter to require a minimum confluence score before any signal is considered valid.
  • Set the Minimum Score Required (out of 9) — default is 5. Only trades meeting or exceeding this threshold are considered. The engine evaluates VWAP position, RSI level, MACD, EMA structure, ADX + price side, Volume, HTF Momentum, and additional factors.
  • Configure the HTF Momentum Timeframe — on a 3-min chart, use 5 minutes or 15 minutes. This checks if the bigger picture agrees with your signal direction, adding an extra layer of confirmation.
  • 💡 Tip: For choppy markets, raise the minimum score to 6/9 or 7/9 to dramatically reduce false signals. Lower it to 4/9 only if you want more frequent (but lower quality) setups.
💰 Risk & Qty Management
Step 7 — How To Manage Risk & Qty Management Per Trade?
Step 7

How To Manage Risk & Qty Management Per Trade? (TP1 to TP5 with Trailing SL)

  • Step 7 — Configure Max Trades Per Day (default 3) and Total Entry Quantity (Lots) (default 3). Enable Auto-Detect Points/Qty Value 4 Indexes for automatic ₹/point detection (NIFTY=₹65, BANKNIFTY=₹30, FINNIFTY=₹60, MIDCAP=₹120), or use Manual ₹ Per Point Override.
  • Set Performance Table Display to P&L (₹) or Points based on your preference.
  • Enable up to 5 Target Levels (TP1–TP5), each with its own exit quantity. Example: enter 3 lots → exit 1 at TP1, 1 at TP2, 1 at TP3. Every partial exit is tracked and labelled on-chart.
  • Trailing SL Options: Move SL to Entry after TP1, Move SL to TP1 after TP3, Move SL to TP2 after TP4. This locks in profits as price moves in your favour.
  • SL & TP Distance (ATR ×) set to 1.5 with ATR Period 14 — so stop loss and target distances adapt to market volatility automatically.
  • 💡 Tip: The sum of all TP quantities must equal your Total Entry Quantity. If they don't match, leftover lots close at session end. Enable Hard Stop Loss for an absolute safety net.
🛡️ Capital Protection Layers
Steps 8A · 8B · 8C · 8D — How To Do Capital Protection?
Steps 8A · 8B · 8C · 8D

How To Do Capital Protection? (Layers 1 to 4)

  • Layer 1 — Daily Loss Limit: Set Max Daily Loss (Points) (e.g., 200). Choose Loss Tracking Method (Gross/Net). Blocks entries if hit.
  • Layer 2 — Consecutive Loss Brake: Set Max Consecutive Losses Before Pause (default 2) and Cooldown Wait (Candles) (default 15). Prevents revenge trading.
  • Layer 3 — Position Size Reduction: Set Reduced Lots After Loss (e.g., 1). Automatically reduces position size after a losing trade.
  • Layer 4 — Raised Confluence Requirement: Set Raise Score If Loss (out of 7) (e.g., 6). Forces higher quality setups after a loss.
  • 💡 Tip: Use the Reset toggles to manually clear protection triggers if you want to resume trading before the next session.
